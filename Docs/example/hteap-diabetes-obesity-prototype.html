CMS Health Tech Ecosystem · Patient-Facing Apps Use Case

Diabetes & Obesity Prevention and Management — Concept Prototype

A patient-facing app that connects to CMS Aligned Networks and, with patient consent, uses the patient's clinical and claims record to deliver personalized coaching, reminders, and risk alerts — adapting to both prevention (pre-diabetes) and active disease management.

The problem

Tens of millions of Medicare beneficiaries live with diabetes, pre-diabetes, or obesity. Today's wellness apps can't see the clinical record, so guidance is generic.

The approach

With patient consent, the app pulls labs, medications, and claims through a CMS Aligned Network (FHIR-based exchange) and turns them into tailored, day-to-day support.

The payoff

Earlier intervention for at-risk patients, better medication adherence, and escalation to the care team when the data signals trouble — not months later at the next visit.

What's happening behind the screen

Every card on the phone is driven by real record data the patient consented to share — not self-reported guesswork. That is the difference a CMS Aligned Network connection makes.

Labs & vitals from the clinical record

A1c, glucose, weight, BMI, and kidney function pulled as FHIR R4 / US Core resources through the Aligned Network — so risk alerts fire on real results, within days of the draw.

Medications & refills from claims

Pharmacy fill history (e.g., Medicare claims data) powers adherence nudges and refill-ready notices — the app knows what was actually dispensed, not just what was prescribed.

Patient-generated data

Steps, logged meals, and connected-device glucose readings combine with the clinical record to personalize day-to-day coaching for prevention or active management.

Consent and identity first

The patient authorizes access through SMART on FHIR / OAuth 2.0 with verified digital identity. Access is scoped, auditable, and revocable by the patient at any time.

Escalation to a human

Per the use-case pledge: the app offers direct assistance when appropriate and directs the patient to a health professional when the data warrants it — like Robert's rising A1c.

One app, two modes

The same platform adapts: prevention mode for pre-diabetic patients (risk scores, MDPP referral, lifestyle goals) and management mode for diagnosed patients (meds, labs, care-team alerts). Toggle the personas above to compare.

How the data flows

The app never scrapes portals or asks patients to fax records. It queries a CMS Aligned Network — a health information network that has pledged to meet the CMS Interoperability Framework criteria — and receives standardized data the patient has consented to share.

Patient access & empowerment

Patients get their full record working for them in a tool they choose — and control the sharing.

Provider access & delegation

Care teams receive escalations and can view patient-shared trends between visits.

Data availability & standards

Clinical and claims data exchanged as FHIR R4 / US Core, aligned to USCDI.

Network connectivity & transparency

Queries ride existing federated network connectivity — no new point-to-point interfaces per provider.

Identity, security & trust

Verified identity, scoped OAuth 2.0 consent, auditable access, patient-revocable at any time.

Security & privacy posture (concept): designed to operate as an unclassified system aligned with the HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules and NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 controls at a FIPS 199 Moderate baseline. Patient data is accessed only under patient-directed consent and never sold or used for advertising. Interface designed to Section 508 / WCAG 2.1 AA.
